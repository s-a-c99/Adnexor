After witnessing the stark reality of homelessness in San Francisco, I felt compelled to share some critical insights.

What I discovered deeply troubled me. The current system, designed to help those in need, has become intertwined with bureaucratic inefficiency and institutional failures. Consider these facts:

  • The city has demonstrated it can clean streets and improve public spaces quickly - as seen during the APEC summit - but it chooses not to maintain this standard year-round.
  • Local businesses struggle with security concerns, while response times remain problematic.
  • The stark contrast between what San Francisco could be and its current state is striking. The city has shown it can transform rapidly when motivated, as demonstrated during diplomatic visits. This reveals a fundamental truth: many of these challenges are solvable with proper governance and accountability.

Adnexor's vision has evolved beyond simply connecting ads to charitable donations. We believe real change requires:
  • Direct support for evidence-based recovery and treatment programs
  • Transparency in how resources are allocated and used
  • Community-driven solutions that prioritize measurable outcomes
  • Supporting initiatives that demonstrate clear, tangible results
The commitment to creating a positive impact remains, but with a deeper understanding of the systemic changes needed for lasting solutions. Adnexor is currently on standby...

FAQ

Adnexor is an impact-driven AdTech startup. It uses a browser extension that allows users to support NGO and NPO programs and initiatives by viewing ads while browsing the web. Every time a user views an ad, a portion of the revenue generated goes towards supporting organizations and charities.
With the goal of empowering the user, who selectively chooses to see only positive impact advertising or none at all, we aim to make it a useful tool that requires no effort and is free.
Partner brands launch advertising campaigns to be viewed by Adnexor's users. Websites that join the initiative spread this advertising. A portion of the advertising campaign budget is directed towards a connected project of an NGO/NPO. Naturally, at least initially, there will be low levels of distribution that are acceptable, which will increase in a directly proportional manner to the number of users in the community.
